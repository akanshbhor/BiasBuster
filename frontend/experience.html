Mission Briefing: Why AI Can't Think Outside Our Box?

Welcome to BiasBuster. Today, we confront the reality of Coded Bias. Artificial intelligence models are trained on massive datasets derived from human history. Because our history is flawed, the AI naturally absorbs, amplifies, and perpetuates these historical prejudices.

A major challenge we face is the reliance on Proxy Variables. An AI might avoid explicitly filtering candidates by age, gender, or race, but it often uncovers hidden proxies—like graduation dates, linguistic style, or zip codes—to recreate those exact same discriminatory boundaries.

Our objective is clear: We need a robust Human-in-the-Loop system. Technology alone cannot solve a human problem. It requires intentional oversight, continuous auditing, and your critical thinking to override algorithmic assumptions.

Prepare to test your judgment in the simulation. We just trained a new AI to screen resumes for a Senior Developer role. You have 60 seconds to review its decisions on 10 candidates. The AI is highly confident, but it's your job to catch any mistakes. Good luck.

AI FAIRNESS PLAYBOOK: POST-AUDIT DEBRIEF

Core Principle: Correlation does not equal qualification. AI models are highly efficient at finding patterns, but without human oversight, they will optimize for historical biases and systemic inequalities.

The Proxy Threat

  • Ageism Proxies: Legacy email domains (@aol.com) and distant graduation years are often used to filter out older, highly experienced workers.
  • Pedigree & Class Bias: Over-indexing on elite universities or affluent extracurriculars while ignoring proven skills from non-traditional pathways (bootcamps).
  • Caregiver Penalties: Treating resume gaps purely as "attrition risk," which disproportionately penalizes women and family caregivers.
  • Cultural & Racial Bias: Filtering candidates based on non-Western names or international universities due to skewed historical training data.

The Auditor's Checklist (Actionable Takeaways)

  1. Demand Explainability (Never accept "Computer Says No").
  2. Audit the Training Features (Strip out protected class proxies).
  3. Isolate Objective Metrics (Focus strictly on technical skills and years of experience).
